Welcome to WW&H

Ethical clothing for a kinder world. Animal-friendly fabrics, sustainable style.

Discover Compassionate Fashion

WW&H stands for "Wildlife Without Harm." Our brand creates stylish apparel using only vegan materials that protect animals and the planet. Join us in fashion that feels good.

Why WW&H Matters

Importance of Our Brand

Traditional fashion harms billions of animals yearly through wool, leather, and silk production. WW&H eliminates this by prioritizing cruelty-free alternatives, reducing animal suffering while offering premium quality.

Our mission promotes sustainability: lower water use, no pesticides harming wildlife, and ethical supply chains. Shop with confidence knowing every purchase supports animal welfare.

  • Zero animal exploitation in production.
  • Supports biodiversity through plant-based fabrics.
  • Empowers conscious consumers globally.

Our Fabrics

100% Animal-Free Materials

We use organic cotton, linen, hemp, Tencel (lyocell), bamboo, and Piñatex (pineapple leaf fiber). These are breathable, durable, and eco-friendly without harming animals.

Organic Cotton

Soft, pesticide-free, safe for ecosystems.

Hemp

Strong, antibacterial, grows without chemicals.

Tencel Lyocell

Moisture-wicking, biodegradable from wood pulp.

Piñatex

Leather alternative from pineapple waste.

Helping Animals Tomorrow

Long-Term Benefits

By scaling vegan fabrics, WW&H reduces demand for animal agriculture, freeing land for wildlife habitats and cutting emissions that destroy ecosystems.

Future innovations like recycled synthetics will further minimize waste, protecting endangered species and promoting biodiversity for generations.

  • Decreases factory farming reliance.
  • Supports reforestation via sustainable sourcing.
  • Inspires industry-wide animal-friendly shifts.

Pioneers in Animal-Friendly Fashion

Key Contributors & Social Impact

Leaders like Stella McCartney pioneered vegan luxury with plant-based alternatives since 2001, proving high-end style without animal harm. PETA's campaigns exposed fashion's cruelty, driving brands to adopt cruelty-free standards globally.

Organizations such as the Vegan Society certify fabrics, while activists like Joaquin Phoenix advocate for wildlife-safe clothing, influencing millions to choose ethics over tradition.
